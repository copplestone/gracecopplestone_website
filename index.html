About

I am an engineer from Bristol, UK. I've had opportunities to travel and work around the world and will be moving back to Europe from the US in the Summer of 2018 to my engineering career.

I completed a Bachelors and Masters degree in Manufacturing Engineering at the University of Cambridge, UK. I have experience as a hands-on rapid prototyping engineer at Hanson Robotics in Hong Kong, the NASA Ames Research Center in California, USA and the Center of Bits and Atoms at Massachusetts Institute of Technology, USA. My work at MIT is documented here, my thesis work is documented here.

In my work I have enjoyed using a range of fabrication techniques including CNC milling, water jet cutting, laser cutting and 3D printing. In addition to this I enjoy managing logistics and supply chain tasks.

Recent Projects

Origami lamp

I used a Zund to score and cut the waterbomb crease pattern into translucent Mylar sheet. After folding this form I used it as a mold within which to cast the base of the lamp.

Gynmastics robot

The MIT Bio-Inspired Robotics class is led by Sangbae Kim. In this class I worked in a team build and control a robot to perform a forward flip.

Standing desk

I recycled the box that a new machine had been delivered in by designing and milling a press fit standing desk.

Bikes

I've been learning how to build and maintain bikes and accessories. I made bags for a mountain biking touring trip and overhauled a 3 speed Phillips bicycle.

Feeds and Speeds calculator

As part of my work in the Fablab network I developed a Feed and Speeds calculator to make it easier for beginner Fablab users to determine settings for CNC milling.

Textiles

I've worked on variety of textiles based projects, most recently I experimented with screen printing on messenger bags and sewed myself a sequin jacket.
